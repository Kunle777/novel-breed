Broiler Feed · Starter Phase

🐣 Broiler Starter Mash

Day-old to 4 weeks · 25kg Bag

22–24%Crude Protein
Day 1 – 4 WksTarget Age
25kgBag Size

What It Does

Broiler Starter Mash is the very first feed for broiler chicks from day-old to approximately 4 weeks. Formulated with very high protein and energy to act as "rocket fuel" — supporting the extremely rapid muscle and skeletal development broilers need in their critical early weeks.

Key Benefits

  • High protein (22–24%) drives rapid early muscle and bone growth
  • Fortified with essential amino acids — lysine and methionine
  • Mash form is easy for young chicks to eat and digest
  • Supports strong immune development in the first weeks of life
  • Fortified with feed additives — no growth hormones

⚠️ Note: Do not feed Broiler Starter to layer chicks for extended periods — the high protein is unnecessary for layers and the feed lacks the calcium they will eventually need.

What It Does

Broiler Grower Mash bridges the gap between starter and finisher phases. It maintains strong growth momentum while shifting the focus toward a balance of protein and energy, supporting lean muscle deposition and strong feed conversion ratios.

Key Benefits

  • Sustains rapid growth from week 4 to 6
  • Balanced protein and energy for lean muscle development
  • Optimised feed conversion ratio (FCR)
  • Fortified with vitamins and minerals for continued health

💡 Tip: Some farmers skip the grower phase and move directly from Starter to Finisher — this is common practice. Speak to our technical team for advice specific to your flock.

Layer Feed · Grower Phase

🌱 Grower Mash

8 to 18 weeks · 25kg Bag

16–18%Crude Protein
8 – 18 WeeksTarget Age
25kgBag Size

What It Does

Grower Mash is the "teenage" feed for pullets aged 8–18 weeks. Its primary role is to deliberately control growth rate to allow proper skeletal development and ensure birds reach sexual maturity at the correct weight — directly determining flock uniformity and lifetime egg output.

Key Benefits

  • Achieves optimum flock uniformity before point of lay
  • Lower calcium than layer feed — prevents kidney damage in young birds
  • Supports reproductive organ development without excess fat gain
  • Higher fibre content helps manage body weight during this phase

⚠️ Important: Do not feed Grower Mash to broilers — it is designed to slow growth, which is the opposite of what broiler farmers need.

Layer Feed · Pre-Layer Phase

🔄 Pre-Layer Mash

16 to 20 weeks · 25kg Bag

17–18%Crude Protein
16 – 20 WeeksTarget Age
~2–2.5%Calcium

What It Does

Pre-Layer Mash is the transition feed between Grower and Layer phases. Its most important job is to provide extra calcium to build bone mineral reserves before the heavy eggshell demands of laying begin. Skipping this stage and moving directly to Layer Mash too early can cause calcium depletion and poor shell quality.

Key Benefits

  • Builds calcium reserves before laying begins
  • More calcium than grower feed, less than full layer feed
  • Helps birds reach point of lay in optimal body condition
  • Reduces risk of early eggshell quality problems

💡 Sign to watch for: Birds approaching lay will show reddening combs or "squatting" behaviour when touched. This is when you transition to Layer Mash.

Layer Feed · Production Phase

🥚 Layer Mash

20 weeks through end of production · 25kg Bag

16–17%Crude Protein
20 Wks – EndTarget Age
3.5–4.5%Calcium

What It Does

Layer Mash is the final and longest-running feed in the layer programme. Fed from 20 weeks through the entire laying cycle, its standout feature is very high calcium content (3.5–4.5%) enabling hens to produce strong, well-formed eggshells daily without depleting their own bone calcium.

Key Benefits

  • High calcium (3.5–4.5%) for consistent, strong eggshell quality
  • Balanced protein maintains hen body weight and sustained production
  • Formulated to maximise eggs per hen housed throughout the cycle
  • Fortified with vitamins A, D3, E and essential minerals

⚠️ Critical Warning: Never feed Layer Mash to broilers. The very high calcium levels can cause permanent kidney damage or death in meat birds — their bodies cannot process this level of calcium.
