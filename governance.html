Our Governance Framework

Built on Accountability, Transparency & Integrity

Novel Breed Agric Limited operates under a formally documented Internal and External Corporate Governance Controls framework — establishing clear structures, roles, responsibilities, and ethical standards that guide every decision we make.

Our governance framework is not just a compliance exercise. It is the foundation of trust we extend to our customers, partners, lenders, and the communities we serve. It defines how we are structured, how decisions get made, and how we hold ourselves accountable.

This framework is aligned with the Companies and Allied Matters Act (CAMA) 2020 and reflects best practices in Nigerian corporate governance.

"Good governance separates management responsibilities from oversight functions to reduce conflicts and enhance accountability — and that principle sits at the heart of how Novel Breed Agric operates."

— Novel Breed Agric Corporate Governance Policy, Version 1
What Guides Us

Core Governance Principles

Six principles underpin every governance decision at Novel Breed Agric Limited.

Separation of Ownership & Management

Clear distinction between ownership, governance, and day-to-day management — ensuring effective oversight and eliminating conflicts of interest at every level.

Ethical Leadership

Directors and managers act with integrity and in the best interests of the company, its shareholders, and all stakeholders — fostering trust, compliance, and long-term success.

Transparency & Disclosure

Timely and accurate disclosure of material information — including financial position, governance practices, and performance — to shareholders and relevant stakeholders.

Fair Treatment of Stakeholders

Equitable rights and access to information for all shareholders, alongside respectful engagement with customers, employees, suppliers, and the broader community.

Risk Management & Internal Control

Proactive identification, assessment, and mitigation of risks affecting company objectives — supported by strong internal controls and an independent audit function.

CAMA 2020 Compliance

Full adherence to the Companies and Allied Matters Act 2020 and all applicable Nigerian laws — ensuring the company operates within the legal framework governing corporate conduct.

Governance Structure

Board & Management Responsibilities

Clear separation of oversight and execution — the Board governs, Management operates.

Board of Directors

  • Approve annual business plan, budget and financial projections
  • Monitor financial performance and operational results
  • Approve major investments and capital expenditures
  • Appoint, evaluate and remunerate senior executives
  • Oversee risk management and internal control systems
  • Ensure integrity of all financial reporting
  • Approve corporate governance policies and codes

Managing Director / CEO

  • Execute Board-approved corporate strategies and policies
  • Manage day-to-day operations, finances and human resources
  • Maintain effective internal controls across all departments
  • Provide regular performance reports to the Board
  • Represent the company to regulators, partners and stakeholders
  • Ensure safety, sustainability and profitability of operations

Audit Committee

  • Review financial statements and accounting policies
  • Supervise and promote financial accountability
  • Monitor effectiveness of internal control systems
  • Recommend appointment and remuneration of external auditors
  • Examine auditor reports and management responses
  • Authorise investigation of areas of concern

Internal Audit & Control

  • Independent function reporting directly to the Board
  • Evaluate adequacy and effectiveness of internal controls
  • Conduct periodic financial, operational and compliance audits
  • Review procurement, inventory, payroll and revenue processes
  • Monitor fraud prevention and detection mechanisms
  • Report findings and recommendations to Management and Board
